PROJECT DESCRIPTION

Drake: By The Numbers is an immersive, horizontally-scrolling data visualization that chronicles Drake's historic journey on the Billboard Hot 100. From his first chart entry in 2009 with "Best I Ever Had" to becoming the most charted artist in Billboard history with 325 entries, this dashboard tells the story of an artist who redefined what chart dominance means.


The visualization features 12 interconnected sections, each exploring different aspects of Drake's career: his early beginnings, the momentum of his rise, his record-breaking album releases, collaboration network, career timeline, streaming statistics, and a comprehensive dashboard summarizing his achievements.

EXPLORATORY DATA ANALYSIS

Before building the visualization, we conducted an exploratory data analysis to understand the dataset's structure, identify patterns, and uncover insights that would inform our visual storytelling.

KEY FINDINGS

Peak Position Distribution

  • 13 songs reached #1 (4% of total)
  • 68 songs peaked at #2-10 (21%)
  • 116 songs peaked at #11-40 (36%)
  • 128 songs peaked at #41-100 (39%)

Longevity Analysis

  • Mean weeks on chart: 10.1
  • Median weeks on chart: 8
  • Standard deviation: 7.2 weeks
  • Range: 1 to 45 weeks

YEARLY TREND ANALYSIS

Drake's chart presence has evolved significantly over his career:

  • 2009-2011: 27 entries (breakthrough era)
  • 2012-2015: 61 entries (consistent growth)
  • 2016-2018: 90 entries (peak dominance)
  • 2019-2025: 147 entries (sustained success)

Peak year: 2020 with 36 chart entries

COLLABORATION PATTERNS

Analysis of Drake's collaborative network:

  • 57% of entries are collaborations
  • 186 unique collaborators identified
  • Top collaborator: Lil Wayne (23 songs)
  • Other frequent partners: Future (20), 21 Savage (17)

Collaborations tend to have slightly higher peak positions on average.
